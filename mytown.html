Ancrum

Ancrum is a small village in the scottish borders with a small population and an interesting history, being right in the middle of historical sites and previously being the area where the Bishop of Glasgow lived. It sits very close to Jedburgh, a Waterloo tower named Peniel Heugh, Fatlips Castle and Monteath Mausoleum.

Picture of Ancrum

Location

Peniel Heugh

Peniel Heugh hill with which sits a Warterloo Monument Watchtower erected between 1817 and 1824, which is 150ft tall and used to have the longest zipline in Britain.

Fatlips Castle

Fatlips Castle was originally built in the 16th century, earning its name by members of the house treating people with less discretion than expected and that "every gentleman, by indefeasible privilege, kisses one of the ladies on entering the ruin."

Monteath Mausoleum

Monteath Mausoleum is a landmark for a crypt that was made in 1864 and supposed to remain sealed. Inside it sits 2 stone angels watching the tomb, and 2 stone lions on guard, one awake and one asleep, laying outside the door.
